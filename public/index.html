Experiment Summary

Survival plots are visual tools used in fuzzing to track the progress of bug or coverage discovery over time, typically plotting time or executions on the x-axis and the number of unique paths, branches, or crashes on the y-axis. They help compare fuzzers by showing how quickly and effectively they discover new issues or code coverage, with plateaus indicating stagnation. Fuzzing techniques vary, with black-box fuzzing using random inputs without knowledge of the program, and white-box fuzzing leveraging symbolic execution and internal analysis for precision. Grey-box fuzzing strikes a balance by using lightweight instrumentation for feedback, while generation-based and mutation-based fuzzers differ in whether they create inputs from scratch or modify existing ones.
